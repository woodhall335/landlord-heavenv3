If the matter is not resolved after service of the Form 3A notice, the claim should be prepared for filing in

The default route in this pack remains the paper county court claim using Form N5 and Form N119.

What To File
File Form N5, Form N119, the notice, and the supporting proof-of-service record together with any
required fee or fee-remission material.
Prepare an indexed bundle containing the tenancy documents, the served notice, service evidence,
and the ground-specific documents relied on.
Updated rent statement and arrears schedule
Payment ledger, bank statements, or rent receipts
Copies of payment demands or arrears correspondence
After Issue
Monitor for any defence, witness evidence, or counterclaim and update the bundle so the response
documents answer it directly.
If circumstances materially change after issue, update the figures or factual chronology promptly and
reflect that change consistently across the pack.
Before the hearing, re-check every court-facing document for blank placeholders, stale dates, or
unsupported allegations.
Ground 8 should only be advanced as a mandatory ground if the arrears still satisfy the threshold of
£3,600.00 at the hearing.
Only treat Possession Claim Online as potentially available if the claim is genuinely confined to eligible
rent-arrears-only Section 8 grounds. Mixed-ground or non-arrears claims should stay on the paper N5
and N119 route.
Do not rely on a stale arrears figure or assume that a mandatory arrears ground still applies if payments
have reduced the balance.
