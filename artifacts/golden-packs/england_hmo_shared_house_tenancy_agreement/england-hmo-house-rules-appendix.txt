2. Communal Living Rules
Cleaning rota or cleaner arrangement: Professional cleaner.
Kitchen, bathroom, and laundry areas should be used considerately and left in a reasonable shared
condition after use.
Guest and overnight guest position: Overnight guests only with prior written approval..
Quiet hours should be set clearly for the shared house.
Waste and recycling arrangement: Bins to be presented every Tuesday night..
3. Safety, Security, and Repairs
Fire safety and escape route note: Do not tamper with detectors and keep escape routes clear..
Do not interfere with fire doors, alarms, detectors, or escape routes.
Keys, fobs, and building access devices should be kept secure and reported promptly if lost.
Repairs affecting shared areas or safety should be reported promptly so the file stays up to date.
Reasonable notice may be given for communal-area inspections, safety checks, and contractor visits.

4. Fair Use and Escalation
Where bills are included, the expectation is fair domestic use. Excessive or unreasonable use may be
raised with the tenant group and should be dealt with reasonably.
Damage to communal areas, repeated cleaning failures, guest breaches, or antisocial behaviour should
be recorded in writing.
Fire safety interference, refusal to cooperate with reasonable inspections, or repeated shared-house
breaches should be escalated in writing and kept on the tenancy file.
Disputes between sharers should be raised promptly so the landlord record shows what issue arose and
what was said or agreed next.
