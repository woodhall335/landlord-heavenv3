2. Shared Home Expectations
General shared-home note: No overnight guests without prior agreement..
Guest and overnight guest expectations should be recorded clearly.
Quiet hours should be agreed clearly.
Kitchen, bathroom, laundry, food storage, and fridge use should be handled respectfully and in line with
the shared-home routine.
Smoking, vaping, alcohol, or drugs should follow the resident-landlord house policy and be dealt with
promptly if an issue arises.
3. Cleaning, Security, and Privacy
The resident landlord and lodger should understand the shared-space cleaning arrangement.
Rubbish and recycling should be dealt with in line with the household arrangements.
Keys should be kept secure and returned at the end of the arrangement.
The resident landlord should explain privacy and room-access expectations clearly as part of the shared-
home arrangement.
Respectful behaviour is expected in the room, shared areas, and around neighbours or other occupiers.

4. Conflict Handling and End of Arrangement
Issues should be raised promptly and a short written note kept where there is a dispute or repeated
problem.
The resident landlord may end the arrangement on notice, and serious misconduct may justify shorter
notice where lawful and reasonable.
At the end of the arrangement the lodger should return keys, clear the room, leave shared areas
reasonably clean, and settle any unpaid licence fees or agreed shared charges.
A forwarding address should be given if the resident landlord may need to send post-arrangement
correspondence.
