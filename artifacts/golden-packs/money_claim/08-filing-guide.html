Important: If the pre-action letter has not gone out properly, or 30 days have not passed, sort that out first. The court can take a poor view of claims that skip the debt protocol.

How to issue the claim

You have two main ways to issue a money claim in England:

  1. Online through Money Claim Online (MCOL) where the claim is suitable for online issue
  2. By post to the County Court Money Claims Centre if you prefer a paper route or the claim needs it

Option 1: File online through MCOL

Why landlords usually choose MCOL

  • Lower court fee: usually cheaper than paper issue
  • Faster issue: claims are often processed more quickly
  • Clear online tracking: easier to follow updates on the case
  • Simpler follow-up steps: some judgment and enforcement requests can also be made online
  • Available at any time: useful if you want to prepare the filing outside court-office hours

Step 6: Wait for issue

After receipt, the court will usually:

  1. Check the papers
  2. Issue the claim and assign a claim number
  3. Serve the claim on the defendant
  4. Send a Notice of Issue

What happens after issue

Typical timeline

Stage What usually happens
Issue and service The claim is issued and served on the defendant
Within 14 days The defendant may pay, admit the claim, defend it, or acknowledge service
After 14 days If there is no response, you may be able to request default judgment
After acknowledgment The defendant usually has up to 28 days from service to file a defence

Likely outcomes

  1. The defendant pays in full and the claim can be brought to an end
  2. The defendant does not respond and you can ask for default judgment
  3. The defendant admits the claim but asks to pay by instalments and you can decide whether to accept the proposal
  4. The defendant files a defence and the court gives directions for the case to move on

If judgment is entered but payment still does not follow

If you obtain judgment and the defendant still does not pay, the next step is enforcement.

  • Warrant of Control if the defendant has goods worth seizing
  • Attachment of Earnings if the defendant is in employment
  • Third Party Debt Order if you know where money is held for the defendant
  • Charging Order if the defendant owns property

Use the Enforcement Guide in this pack to decide which route best matches the defendant's circumstances.
