  • If service is challenged, be ready to explain why the method chosen complied with the tenancy agreement and why the service date used in the pack is the correct date.

Validity Checks

Before You Rely On The Notice
  • Check that the same party names, address, grounds, court name, and notice timeline appear consistently across the Form 3A notice, the support documents, and any court forms.
  • Ground 8 should only be advanced as a mandatory ground if the arrears still satisfy the threshold of £3,600.00 at the hearing.
  • If arrears grounds are relied on, make sure the arrears schedule and the pleaded figures still match the current rent account.

Notice periods: The required notice period depends on the grounds relied on and can change. If you alter the grounds, the service method, or the intended service date, regenerate the pack so every document uses the same recalculated dates.

After Service

Do Not Do These Things
  • Review the pack as a whole before issue so that dates, court details, and ground wording remain aligned.
  • Remove empty or unused sections rather than leaving placeholder text or blank visual gaps in court-facing documents.

Risk warning: A possession claim can be delayed or contested if the notice dates, service evidence, or grounds particulars are inconsistent across the pack, even where the underlying arrears or breach evidence is strong.
