Suggested wording for a landlord reply if the tenant asks the tribunal to determine the market rent.
The recorded comparables support the proposed rent and place it below the adjusted median of
£1297.50 pcm.
Property: 10 Sample Road, Leeds, LS1 1AA
Suggested response
I am the landlord or authorised agent for 10 Sample Road, Leeds, LS1 1AA. Form 4A was served on 25
March 2026 proposing a rent of £1285.00 per month from 01 June 2026.
The tenancy is currently charged at £1200.00 per month. The notice date and proposed start date were
selected so the rent review timetable recorded in the file is met.
The landlord relies on the comparable evidence exhibited with the justification report. The analysis shows
an adjusted median market rent of £1297.50 per month, with the proposed rent sitting below the adjusted
median of £1297.50 pcm.
The comparable schedule uses nearby properties of similar size and adjusts them to a monthly equivalent
so that like is compared with like. The proposal is based on that market evidence, not on a target
percentage increase from the historic rent alone.
The landlord therefore asks the tribunal to determine that the proposed rent is supported by the market
evidence, or alternatively to set the market rent at the nearest figure supported by the exhibited
comparables.
Points to confirm in any witness statement or reply
1. Service method and service date: First class post on 25 March 2026.
2. Proposed start date: 01 June 2026.
3. Comparable base: 8 comparable two-bedroom properties within 0.5 miles, with all listed within the last 90
days and all source-backed.
4. Current banding: challenge risk is Moderate likelihood of challenge and evidence strength is Strong
evidence.
5. Any manual adjustment or override should be tied back to a recorded factual difference in the
comparable schedule and not left as an unexplained preference.
Evidence to attach
Attach the completed Form 4A, proof of service record, justification report, Tribunal Argument Summary,
relevant exhibits, and any correspondence showing attempts to resolve the issue before a hearing.
If photos, EPC records, or tenancy documents are relied on, label them consistently with the bundle index
so the tribunal can move from the statement to the exhibit without confusion.
