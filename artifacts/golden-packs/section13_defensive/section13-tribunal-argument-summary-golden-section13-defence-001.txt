Argument in brief
The proposal should be judged by reference to nearby open-market comparables, not by the size of
the increase from the previous rent alone.
The landlord's position is that 8 comparable two-bedroom properties within 0.5 miles, with all listed
within the last 90 days and all source-backed supports a proposed rent of £1285.00 per month, which
remains below the adjusted median of £1297.50 pcm and should therefore be judged against the
recorded comparable market evidence rather than the size of the increase alone.
Recorded position: The proposed rent of £1285.00 per month is below the adjusted median market
rent of £1297.50 per month. Based on the comparables analysed under section 13(4) of the Housing
Act 1988 (as amended), the proposed increase is presented as reasonable with a moderate likelihood
of challenge and strong evidence strength. The proposed rent reflects recent local listings, condition,
and current market positioning for similar two-bedroom homes.
Strength of the evidence
Evidence assessment: Strong evidence strength is based on 8 comparable homes, 8 source-backed
entries, and 8 comparables dated within the 90-day freshness window. The file reads as a
well-supported market review rather than a bare assertion.
Comparable 1: Comparable 1, Leeds at £1240.00 monthly, adjusting to £1240.00 pcm, dated 15 March
2026.
Comparable 2: Comparable 2, Leeds at £1265.00 monthly, adjusting to £1265.00 pcm, dated 15 March
2026.
