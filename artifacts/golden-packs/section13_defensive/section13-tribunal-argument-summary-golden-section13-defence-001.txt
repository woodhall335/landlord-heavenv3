Tribunal Argument Summary
This is the landlord's concise argument brief for the hearing file.
The proposed rent of £1285.00 per month is supported by current comparable evidence and sits
below the adjusted median of £1297.50 pcm.
Adjusted median market rent: £1297.50 pcm
Argument in brief
The proposal should be judged by reference to nearby open-market comparables, not by the size of the
increase from the previous rent alone.
The landlord relies on the comparable evidence in this file and says the proposed rent of £1285.00 per
month should be judged against that market evidence, not against the size of the increase alone.
The proposed rent of £1285.00 per month is below the adjusted median market rent of £1297.50 per
month. Based on the comparables analysed under section 13(4) of the Housing Act 1988 (as amended),
the proposed increase is presented as reasonable with a moderate likelihood of challenge and strong
evidence strength. The proposed rent reflects recent local listings, condition, and current market positioning
for similar two-bedroom homes.
Strength of the evidence
Strong evidence strength is based on 8 comparable homes, 8 source-backed entries, and 8 comparables
dated within the 90-day freshness window. The file reads as a well-supported market review rather than a
bare assertion.
Comparable 1: Comparable 1, Leeds at £1240.00 monthly, adjusting to £1240.00 pcm, dated 15 March
2026.
Comparable 2: Comparable 2, Leeds at £1265.00 monthly, adjusting to £1265.00 pcm, dated 15 March
2026.
If challenged, say this
1. Anchor to evidence
Say: The proposed rent is based on comparable local properties and the recorded adjustments, not on a
percentage increase alone.
2. Position in the range
Say: The figure sits below the adjusted median of £1297.50 pcm and should be judged against the adjusted
comparable range recorded in the report.
3. Strength of evidence
Say: The proposal is supported by 8 source-backed comparables, with 8 listings inside the 90-day
freshness window.
4. Bundle integrity
Say: The full bundle includes the notice, service record, justification report, Tribunal Argument Summary,
and the supporting documents I rely on.
Important limit
This summary is a concise argument brief drawn from the saved case facts and comparable analysis. It
supports presentation of the case, but it does not guarantee a tribunal outcome.
