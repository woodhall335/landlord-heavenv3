Court Filing Guide

England private rented possession claims on and after 1 May 2026

Generated: 05/04/2026

Overview

This guide explains the court stage after serving Form 3A in England's private rented sector.

  • PCOL may be used if the claim is only for rent arrears.
  • Paper filing with Form N5 and Form N119 is the standard route for non-rent or mixed-ground claims and can also be used for rent-only claims if preferred.
Which court? File at the County Court that covers the area where the property is located. Find your court at www.gov.uk/find-court-tribunal.
Fees and official forms: The current County Court recovery-of-land issue fee is GBP 404 for both PCOL and paper possession claims. This pack is mapped to the current official GOV.UK N5 and N119 PDFs; check GOV.UK again immediately before filing in case HMCTS publishes an updated form or fee.

Option 1: PCOL For Rent-Only Claims

Possession Claims Online (PCOL) can be used if you are seeking possession only because the tenant owes rent.

Eligibility for PCOL

  • the claim is only for rent arrears possession grounds
  • the property is residential and has a valid UK postcode
  • there are no more than 2 defendants
  • you can pay the fee online

1 Create an account

Register at www.possessionclaim.gov.uk.

2 Prepare your evidence

Have the tenancy information, Form 3A notice details, proof of service, and up to 2 years of rent due and rent paid data ready. Check first that the tenant is not in a breathing space if you rely on Grounds 8, 10 or 11.

3 Upload the supporting documents

Include the tenancy agreement, the completed Form 3A notice, proof of service, and the arrears schedule or rent statement. If the tenant receives Universal Credit housing support, keep any DWP direct-payment request or deduction correspondence with the bundle.

4 Pay the fee and submit

Possession claims currently cost GBP 404. Once submitted, you will receive a claim number and the court will serve the claim on the tenant.

Option 2: Paper Filing With N5 And N119

Documents Required

  • Form N5 claim form
  • Form N119 particulars of claim
  • Copy of the tenancy agreement
  • Copy of the served Form 3A notice
  • Proof of service
  • Arrears schedule if rent grounds are used
  • Supporting evidence for each ground relied on
  • Optional certificate of service (N215)
Give the court at least one extra copy of each document. If the tenancy is joint, provide an extra copy for each tenant and keep a copy for yourself.

Filing Steps

1 Complete Form N5 and Form N119

Your Landlord Heaven complete pack includes both forms ready for review and signature.

2 Send the papers to court

Post, email, or deliver the papers to the County Court that covers the property. Check local court rules before filing by email.

3 Pay the court fee

Application Current fee
Possession claim GBP 404

4 Wait for issue and hearing date

The court will issue the claim, serve it on the tenant, and list the possession hearing.

What Happens Next

  1. The court serves the claim on the tenant.
  2. The tenant usually has 14 days to file a defence.
  3. The court lists a possession hearing.
  4. You attend with the notice, proof of service, and evidence for each ground.
  5. The judge decides whether to make a possession order, a suspended order, a money order, or dismiss the claim.
A claim can be dismissed or delayed if the Form 3A notice is incomplete, the notice period is too short, the deposit rules were not satisfied where they apply, service cannot be proved, or the evidence does not match the grounds relied on.

Pre-Hearing Preparation

  • Bring the sealed claim, tenancy agreement, Form 3A, proof of service, and the latest arrears schedule.
  • Be ready to explain the rent history, the notice date, the notice period, and the evidence for each ground relied on.
  • If the tenant offers a repayment plan or a suspended possession order, decide in advance what terms you would accept.
  • Review any defence filed by the tenant and bring documents that answer it directly, especially on deposit compliance, disrepair, service, and the arrears figure.

Money Judgment And Debt Recovery

Where you seek possession on rent arrears grounds, the court can also make a money judgment for arrears, issue fee, and any costs it allows.

If the tenant leaves before the hearing or after judgment, keep the arrears schedule updated. You may still need to pursue the debt separately if the possession claim no longer continues or if the arrears change materially.
If the tenant is in a breathing space, do not serve or progress rent-arrears enforcement without following the debt respite rules or obtaining the court's permission where required.

After The Possession Order

If the tenant does not leave by the date in the order, you can apply for enforcement.

1 Request a warrant for possession

Use Form N325 for an outright possession order, or Form N325A if a suspended possession order has been breached. The current warrant fee is GBP 148.

2 Bailiff enforcement

The court will list the bailiff appointment. Have a locksmith ready for the eviction date if enforcement is granted.

Tip: Keep the notice, claim papers, service evidence, and possession order together for the enforcement stage.
