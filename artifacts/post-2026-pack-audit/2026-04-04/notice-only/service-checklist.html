2 Service Evidence Checklist

Evidence of Service

Confirm you have collected and retained the following evidence of service:

  • Original signed Form 3A notice
  • Copy of the notice served to tenant
  • Date and time of service recorded
  • Method of service documented (hand delivery / first class post / recorded delivery)
  • Proof of service:
    • If hand delivered: Photo of letterbox with notice visible, witness statement
    • If posted: Proof of postage receipt from Royal Mail
    • If recorded delivery: Tracking number and delivery confirmation
  • Witness details recorded (name, address, signature) if applicable
  • Any correspondence from tenant acknowledging receipt

⚠️ Keep All Evidence

You must prove service in court. Without clear evidence, your claim may be dismissed even if the notice is otherwise valid.

3 Validity Requirements

Notice Validity (Housing Act 1988 section 8 / Form 3A)

Verify your notice meets these validity requirements:

  • Notice uses the correct official form (Form 3A)
  • All mandatory fields completed (landlord name/address, tenant name, property address)
  • Ground(s) for possession clearly stated with particulars
  • Section 16E duties checked: no sham fixed term, no notice to quit or oral termination, no purported notice, and each ground relied on is genuinely available on the facts
  • Where a prior-notice ground is used, confirm the written statement under section 16D identified that ground before the tenancy started or within the statutory 28-day window where applicable
  • Notice period is sufficient for the grounds claimed:
    • 4 months minimum: Grounds such as 1, 1A, 2, 2ZA to 2ZD, 4A, 6 and 6B
    • 2 months minimum: Grounds such as 5, 5A, 5B, 5C, 5H, 7 and 9
    • 4 weeks minimum: Grounds 5E, 5F, 5G, 8, 10 and 11
    • 2 weeks minimum: Grounds 7B, 12, 13, 14A, 14ZA, 15 and 17
    • Immediate application route: Grounds 7A and 14 allow immediate application, but the notice still needs to be completed and served properly
    • Mixed grounds: Use the longest statutory notice period among all grounds claimed
  • Periodic tenancy: Earliest court date is the day after the statutory notice period expires, counted from the date of service
  • Breathing space checked: if the tenant is in a Debt Respite Scheme breathing space you must not serve or progress rent-arrears grounds without court permission
  • Notice signed and dated by landlord or landlord's agent
  • Discretionary ground(s) claimed: Court will consider whether it is reasonable to grant possession, taking into account all circumstances including tenant's personal situation.
  • Ground 1A reminder: after relying on the sale ground, keep clear sale evidence and do not market or re-let during the section 16E restricted period except where a statutory exception applies

4 After Service

Post-Service Actions

  • Wait until possession date has passed: 01/10/2026
  • Do not change locks or remove tenant belongings (illegal eviction)
  • Do not harass tenant or cut off utilities
  • If tenant remains after possession date, apply to court using Form N5 (standard possession claim)
  • Keep proof of service readily accessible for court
