4 Grounds for Possession

I am seeking possession of the property relying on Ground 8 and Ground 10 and Ground 11 of Schedule 2 to the Housing Act 1988. Ground 8 is a mandatory ground for possession. Ground 8 requires that at least 3 months' rent be unpaid both at the date of the notice and at the date of the hearing. At the date of service of the Form 3A notice (1 June 2026), the total rent arrears stood at £4,200.00, representing approximately 3.5 months' rent. This significantly exceeds the Ground 8 threshold of 3 months' rent (£3,600.00). As at the date of this statement (26 April 2026), the total rent arrears amount to £4,200.00, representing approximately 3.5 months of unpaid rent. A detailed Schedule of Arrears (Exhibit TAM1) showing the period-by-period breakdown of rent due and payments received is attached to this pack. I will provide an updated Schedule of Arrears to the court before the hearing to confirm the arrears position at that date. Ground 10 is a discretionary ground which applies where some rent is unpaid both at the date of the notice and at the date of proceedings. The court has discretion to make a possession order if it considers it reasonable to do so. Ground 11 is a discretionary ground which applies where the tenant has persistently delayed paying rent, even if no rent is currently outstanding. The court has discretion to make a possession order if it considers it reasonable to do so.
