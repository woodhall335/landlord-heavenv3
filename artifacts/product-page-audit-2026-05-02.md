## Competition Comparison

### Section 8

Compared with Landlord Action's Section 8 page and timeline pages, our Section 8 pages are now:

- clearer on which route to buy
- better at showing the landlord where they are in the process
- stronger on preview-led trust
- stronger at explaining the product as a system rather than a service contact page

Landlord Action is still stronger on raw legal authority and urgency. Its copy is blunter and sounds more solicitor-backed, but it is also more advisory-service led than product led.

### Section 13

Compared with Lawpack's rent increase page:

- our Standard Section 13 page is materially stronger on route fit, evidence framing, and preview proof
- our Defence page is in a different league as a premium product proposition
- Lawpack stays simpler and cheaper, but it feels like a form plus booklet, not a landlord-ready evidence pack

### Tenancy

Compared with Lawpack tenancy pages and Simply-Docs tenancy pages:

- our Standard and Premium pages are more commercially guided and easier to self-qualify from
- our specialist Student, HMO, and Lodger pages are much more differentiated than Lawpack's category-level structure
- Simply-Docs sounds more technical and reference-led, but much less persuasive as a buy-now product page

Lawpack still sounds plainer in a few places because it says less. Our pages now generally say more useful things without sounding as stiff as they did before, which is a better commercial trade-off.

## Bottom-Line Position

- Better than template/catalogue competitors on clarity, route choice, and perceived product depth.
- Closer to a specialist landlord brand on Section 8 and Section 13 than on the specialist tenancy pages.
- Still not as bluntly authoritative as a solicitor-led sales page on eviction matters, but stronger as a self-serve product system.
