# Implementation Plan

## What Was Implemented

1. Added a small, reusable article shell for HR-overlap editorial pages.
2. Added three carefully placed HRHeaven links to existing pages where the staffing context is natural.
3. Created five support articles requested in the master prompt.
4. Added the five new pages to the sitemap.
5. Produced this rollout audit folder to document the link programme.

## Recommended First Deployment

Deploy the current conservative set first:

- 3 existing-page editorial links.
- 5 new support articles.
- 11 total HRHeaven links.

This is enough to create a real topical bridge without looking like a network-wide SEO manoeuvre.

## Future Expansion Rules

- Add future links manually only after page-by-page review.
- Keep max 1 HRHeaven link on existing pages.
- Keep max 2 HRHeaven links on new HR-overlap articles.
- Do not add HRHeaven links to global templates, sidebars, footers, or generated documents.
- Prefer broader 200-status destination URLs over redirecting long-tail destinations unless HRHeaven canonical routes are confirmed.
- Avoid repetitive anchors.

## Pages To Consider Later

- A dedicated article for landlords hiring caretakers.
- A dedicated article for cleaners in HMOs and shared houses.
- A guide for letting agency admin processes and tenant data handling.
- A guide for property businesses offboarding staff with keys and tenant data access.

## What Not To Do

- Do not create a sitewide "HRHeaven" footer link.
- Do not inject links into all blog pages automatically.
- Do not use repeated exact-match anchors.
- Do not link HRHeaven from legal forms, dashboards, checkout, or customer documents.
