# Pages Skipped

The programme deliberately did not add every possible backlink. Pages were skipped where the HRHeaven connection would be weak, repetitive, too template-driven, or too close to legal process pages where an HR link would distract from the landlord action.

| Page or page type | Reason skipped |
|---|---|
| `/tenant-refusing-access` | The page is rendered from shared high-intent content data. It has a possible caretaker/property-manager overlap, but placing an HR link in the shared content layer carried more risk than value in this pass. |
| Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland tenancy pages | The HRHeaven destinations are UK employment-focused, but this backlink pass was intended to support England landlord/property business content first. |
| Wizard, checkout, dashboard, account, admin, and document viewer pages | Explicitly excluded by the implementation prompt. These are transactional or private flows, not editorial backlink surfaces. |
| Generated PDFs and document templates | Explicitly excluded. Customer documents must not carry SEO backlinks. |
| Footer, header, navigation, sidebars, reusable CTAs, and global templates | Explicitly excluded to avoid sitewide or manipulative link patterns. |
| Most Section 8 ground pages | Most grounds do not naturally discuss staff or HR. Ground 16 was created because employment-linked accommodation is genuinely relevant. |
| Court possession pages such as `/n5-n119-possession-claim` | The user intent is court filing and possession documents. HR links would be peripheral unless the specific case concerns employment-linked accommodation. |
| Generic rent arrears and possession pages | These pages should pass intent to Landlord Heaven notice, complete pack, and money claim products rather than introduce HRHeaven links. |
