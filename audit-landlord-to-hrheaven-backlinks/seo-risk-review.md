# SEO Risk Review

Audit status: recommendations only. No links added.

## Final Risk Rating

Overall risk is low if links are:

- editorial
- sparse
- relevant to the page topic
- varied in anchor text
- placed in body copy rather than global navigation
- limited to pages where a landlord or property-business reader genuinely benefits

Risk becomes medium or high if Landlord Heaven links to HRHeaven from many unrelated eviction, rent, or court pages.

## Main SEO Risks

### 1. Sitewide cross-domain link pattern

Risk:

Adding HRHeaven to the footer, sidebar, navigation, wizard side panel, or global components would create many repeated links.

Recommendation:

Do not add sitewide HRHeaven links.

### 2. Repetitive exact-match anchors

Risk:

Using the same anchor such as `property manager employment contract` on many Landlord Heaven pages may look engineered.

Recommendation:

Rotate plain anchors and use broader anchors where the page topic is broader.

### 3. Irrelevant page-level context

Risk:

Linking from legal/eviction pages where HR is not discussed can look manipulative and may reduce trust.

Recommendation:

Only link where the paragraph itself discusses staff, property managers, cleaners, caretakers, admin support, letting agencies, employees, or contractor status.

### 4. Over-commercial linking

Risk:

Every link pointing to a product-like HRHeaven page may look purely promotional.

Recommendation:

Mix destination intent:

- `/industry/real-estate`
- `/employment-contract`
- `/employee-handbook`
- `/hr-document-pack`
- specific contract pages only when context is specific.

### 5. Jurisdiction/product mismatch

Risk:

Some Landlord Heaven non-England tenancy pages appear to exist, but previous product strategy may have retired or reduced those flows. Linking from retired or inconsistent pages could amplify weak pages.

Recommendation:

Do not use Wales, Scotland, or Northern Ireland tenancy pages for HRHeaven links until those pages are confirmed active, accurate, and commercially useful.

### 6. User trust risk

Risk:

A landlord reading about eviction or money claim documents may be confused by an unrelated HR link.

Recommendation:

Use a short, transparent transition sentence:

> If you also employ staff in your property business, keep that employment paperwork separate from the tenancy or court file.

## Safe Link Density

Recommended:

- Existing pages: 1 HRHeaven link per page.
- New HR-overlap articles: 1 or 2 HRHeaven links per page if naturally separated by topic.
- Whole Landlord Heaven site initial phase: 5 to 8 links.

Avoid:

- more than 2 HRHeaven links on any page
- more than 1 link in any repeated template block
- links in global components

## Safest Destinations

1. `https://hrheaven.co.uk/industry/real-estate`
2. `https://hrheaven.co.uk/industry/real-estate/property-manager-employment-contract`
3. `https://hrheaven.co.uk/industry/cleaning/cleaner-employment-contract`
4. `https://hrheaven.co.uk/employment-contract`
5. `https://hrheaven.co.uk/employee-handbook`

## Red Flags To Avoid

- linking from every “cleaning” mention
- linking from every “agent” mention
- using HR links inside checkout/wizard flow
- exact-match anchor repetition
- brand-heavy wording such as “visit HRHeaven for the best HR documents”
- hidden, footer, sidebar, or boilerplate links

## Recommended Monitoring

After implementation:

- track indexed source pages
- monitor HRHeaven referral traffic
- monitor HRHeaven target page impressions
- check whether anchors look naturally varied
- review Search Console after 4 to 8 weeks
- avoid adding more links until initial effects are visible
