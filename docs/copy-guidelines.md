# Copy guidelines - Landlord Heaven

## Who we're writing for
A UK private landlord with a real problem right now.
Not a lawyer. Not a property professional. Usually short on time and under pressure.

## Voice in one sentence
Write like a knowledgeable, practical friend who understands the process and explains it clearly.

## Core rule
Start with the problem. Show the next step. Explain why it helps.

## Hard rules
- Use "you" and "your"
- Name the problem before the solution
- Use plain English, not internal jargon
- Make every feature answer "why does this matter?"
- Make CTAs specific and action-led
- Remove any visible strategy or SEO language
- Weave SEO in naturally, never force it

## Avoid
workflow, outputs, reform-aware, statutory-grounded, jurisdiction-specific, use case, landlord journey, topic cluster, authority hub, conversion, commercial spine, thin content

## Prefer
your situation, your case, what you need to do, for England, for Scotland, the right document for your situation, checks to help you avoid mistakes

## SEO approach
Use target keywords naturally in headings, intros, and body copy where they fit.
Do not force exact-match phrases where they make the copy worse.
Write for clarity first, search second.

## Safety rules
- Do not invent facts, prices, legal claims, or statistics
- Do not make copy sound more legally certain than the source material
- Do not add legal advice
- Match the disclaimer and confidence level of the source copy

## What good looks like
GOOD: "Your tenant owes rent and has stopped replying. Here's the next step to start getting your property back."

BAD: "Landlord Heaven provides jurisdiction-specific document generation with reform-aware compliance outputs."
